Evidence released after grand jury refuses indictment

On August 9, 2014, Ferguson police officer Darren Wilson shot and killed Michael Brown, an unarmed black teenager.

Eleven days later, a St. Louis County grand jury began hearing evidence on whether or not to charge Wilson with a crime for Brown’s death.

On November 24, St. Louis County prosecutor Bob McCulloch announced that the nine white and three African-American members had decided that Wilson did not commit a crime when he killed Brown.

As he promised from the beginning if there was no indictment, McCulloch has made the evidence available for public inspection, and St. Louis Public Radio now presents it to you. We hope it helps you answer two questions – what happened that August day, and why wasn’t Darren Wilson charged with a crime?
